Principal Investigator

Yu-Ru Lin

Assistant Professor at the School of Computing and Information, University of Pittsburgh. She is a computer scientist by training, with research interests include data science, visualization, and computational social science. She has worked extensively on computational methodologies for mining, analyzing and visualizing large-scale, time-varying, heterogeneous, multi-relational, and semi-structured data. Her current research focuses on tracking and modeling collective behavior from big data sets, including data from social media and sensing technologies, for understanding human and social dynamics, particularly under exogenous events including disasters.
